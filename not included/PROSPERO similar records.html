  8. Prediction models for longitudinal patterns in depression and anxiety: A systematic review [published 18 December 2024]

    "What prediction models exist for predicting longitudinal patterns of depression and/or anxiety in the general population, and how do the models compare with regards to model characteristics and performance?"
    The overall aim of this systematic review is to examine prediction models of longitudinal patterns in anxiety and/or depression.
    We use the term prediction model to refer to studies aimed at developing, validating, and adjusting (e.g. extending) multivariable prediction/prognostic models that include multiple prediction/prognostic factors combined, and are to be used for making predictions in individuals.
    We use the terms longitudinal patterns or trajectories in this protocol to encompass all studies using longitudinal modelling of repeated anxiety and/or depression to quantitatively or qualitatively model change over time.
    Objectives:
    1. Systematically identify literature on prediction models for predicting longitudinal depression and anxiety patterns.
    2. Review key model characteristics including predictors included, model development, validation and performance.
    3. To assess the quality and risk of bias in existing reports.
    4. To make recommendations for development of prediction models for depression/anxiety trajectories in future.
